Big Data Visualization Methods

Handling Big Data is one of the notable hurdles of the 21st century because of the large volume (Volume), inconsistency of data and multiple formats (Variety), and high computing speed (Velocity). But it is important to analyze Big Data to gain insights into multiple fields and discover research opportunities. Data visualization relies on human cognitive capacities to absorb visual information, making it a solution to facilitate meaningful analysis, accessibility, and interpretation of huge data. The goal is to compare visualization methods to see which of them satisfy all the Big Data criteria.

Population

As of 2019, the world population is estimated to be 7 billion and is predicted to rise. This rise in population is attributed to many factors such as medical advancements and rise in agricultural productivity which has decreased mortality rate and famine all over the world.

Life Expentancy and Health Expenditure per Capita ($)

There is a positive correlation between Life Expentancy and Health Expenditure which means that countries that invest more money in healthcare are likely to have residents with longer life expectancy. It is also notable that developed countries have higher expenditure and life expectancy unlike developing countries which means that more effort has to be put in to improve the heathcare systems of developing countries. Note that India and China have high life expectancies but low expenditure per capita due to their high population.

Life Expentancy and Fertility Rate

It is interesting to note that life expectancy increases with a decrease in fertility rate. This can be due to the fact that the energetic and metabolic costs from reproduction weakens the human body which increases fatality rate. Also, developed countries have higher life expentancy and lower fertility rate than developing countries. This can be due to increased literacy rate which involves women knowning and using more about contraceptives and other birth control options. Also women in these countries may have less societal burden to have children and can devote more time to themselves.

Surgical Workforce and Physicians

It can be noticed that Low Income and Low Middle Income have severe shortages of specialised surgeons and physicans which explains low life expectancies often due to deaths among infants and newborns. The Lancet Comission on Global Surgery recommends at least 20 surgical workers per 10,1000 people and WHO recommends 4.5 physicans per 1,000 people. More funding have to be given to healthcare systems of these countries so that their residents get access to immaculate healthcare.
